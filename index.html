About Me

I am a 69-year-old retired entrepreneur, born, raised, and based in Tel Aviv. A widower, father of four, and grandfather of four, I am now focused on contributing my time, energy, and accumulated wisdom to pro bono initiatives—supporting individuals, small organizations, and associations in realizing their goals with clarity and purpose.

With decades of experience initiating, managing, and promoting projects across technical, commercial, and interpersonal domains, I have often been called upon to provide tailored guidance in areas such as negotiation, project management, and sales—whether of assets, services, or abstract ideas.

Beyond my professional achievements, I consider myself an intellectual in the original sense: someone drawn to ideas with no immediate utility. I am deeply curious about politics, relationships, psychology, sports, and human behavior—areas that shape lives and societies, even if their outcomes can't always be measured.

At this stage of life, I'm not seeking compensation—only engagement. I'm eager to mentor, advise, and support others who are building, struggling, or searching, and I bring to those interactions a spirit of generosity, clarity, and sincere curiosity.
